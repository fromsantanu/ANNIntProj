## Chapter 2: Problem Statement

In this chapter, we clearly define **what problem we are solving** and **what our model is expected to do**.

---

### 🎯 The Core Problem

A company wants to reduce customer loss.

Right now, they don’t know:

> “Which customers are likely to leave soon?”

So our task is:

👉 Build a system that can **predict customer churn in advance**

---

### 🧠 Problem in Simple Words

We are trying to answer:

> “Given a customer’s details, can we predict if they will stay or leave?”

---

### 📥 Input (What We Give to the Model)

Each customer will have some information like:

* Age
* Monthly bill amount
* Type of contract
* Number of complaints
* Internet usage
* Payment method

👉 Think of this as a **profile of the customer**

---

### 📤 Output (What Model Should Predict)

The model will give a simple output:

* **0 → Customer will stay**
* **1 → Customer will leave**

👉 This is called **binary classification** (only two possible outcomes)

---

### 🔍 Why This Problem Matters

If the company can predict churn early:

* They can offer discounts
* Improve service
* Contact the customer

👉 This helps **retain customers and save revenue**

---

### 🧩 How ANN Fits Here

Instead of manually guessing, we use an ANN built with PyTorch.

The ANN will:

* Look at many customer examples
* Learn patterns automatically
* Predict for new customers

---

### 📌 Formal Definition (Simple Version)

We want to learn a function:

> **Customer Data → Churn Prediction**

Or in simple terms:

👉 Input → Model → Output

---

### 🧪 Example

| Age | Bill | Complaints | Contract | Output    |
| --- | ---- | ---------- | -------- | --------- |
| 45  | High | 3          | Monthly  | 1 (Leave) |
| 30  | Low  | 0          | Yearly   | 0 (Stay)  |

👉 The model learns from many such examples.

---

### 🚀 Final Goal of This Project

By the end, we will have a model that can:

✔ Take a new customer’s data
✔ Predict churn probability
✔ Help in decision-making
