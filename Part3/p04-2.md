## Chapter 10.2: Precision and Recall

Accuracy gives a general idea, but it is often **not enough**, especially in problems like churn prediction.

👉 That’s why we use **Precision** and **Recall**

---

### 🧠 Why Do We Need These?

In churn prediction:

* Predicting a customer will leave → important decision
* Mistakes can be costly

👉 We need deeper insight than just accuracy

---

## 🔹 1. Precision

### 🧠 Definition

Precision means:

> “Out of all customers predicted to leave, how many actually left?”

---

### 📊 Formula

$\text{Precision} = \frac{TP}{TP + FP}$

Where:

* TP = True Positive (correctly predicted churn)
* FP = False Positive (predicted churn but actually stayed)

---

### 🧪 Example

* Model predicted 20 customers will leave
* Actually, 15 left

👉 Precision = 15 / 20 = **75%**

---

### 🎯 Interpretation

✔ High precision means:

* When model says “leave”, it is usually correct

---

## 🔹 2. Recall

### 🧠 Definition

Recall means:

> “Out of all customers who actually left, how many did we correctly identify?”

---

### 📊 Formula

$\text{Recall} = \frac{TP}{TP + FN}$

Where:

* FN = False Negative (customer left but model missed it)

---

### 🧪 Example

* Total customers who left = 20
* Model correctly predicted = 15

👉 Recall = 15 / 20 = **75%**

---

### 🎯 Interpretation

✔ High recall means:

* Model catches most churn customers

---

## ⚖️ Precision vs Recall

| Metric    | Focus                          |
| --------- | ------------------------------ |
| Precision | Correctness of predicted churn |
| Recall    | Coverage of actual churn       |

---

### ⚠️ Trade-Off

Usually:

* Increasing precision → lowers recall
* Increasing recall → lowers precision

👉 We must balance both

---

### ⚙️ Python Code Example

```python id="k3f1bp"
from sklearn.metrics import precision_score, recall_score

y_pred = (model(X_test) > 0.5).float()

precision = precision_score(y_test, y_pred)
recall = recall_score(y_test, y_pred)

print("Precision:", precision)
print("Recall:", recall)
```

---

### 📈 Real-Life Analogy

Think of a disease test:

* Precision → when test says “disease”, how often correct
* Recall → how many actual patients were detected

👉 Missing a patient (low recall) can be dangerous

---

### 🚀 Key Takeaways

✔ Precision focuses on correctness
✔ Recall focuses on coverage
✔ Both are important for real-world problems
✔ Must be balanced carefully
