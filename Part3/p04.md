## Chapter 10: Model Evaluation

After training and validation, we now answer the most important question:

👉 **How good is our model?**

This step is called **Model Evaluation**.

---

### 🧠 What is Model Evaluation?

Model evaluation means:

> “Measuring how well the model performs on unseen data”

---

### 🎯 Why is This Important?

A model is useful only if it works well on **new data**.

👉 Not just the data it has already seen.

---

### 📊 What Do We Measure?

We don’t rely on just one measure.
We use multiple **evaluation metrics**:

* Accuracy
* Precision
* Recall
* F1 Score

---

## 🔹 1. Accuracy

Accuracy means:

> “How many predictions are correct out of total predictions”

---

### 🧪 Example

* Total customers = 100
* Correct predictions = 90

👉 Accuracy = 90%

---

### ⚠️ Problem with Accuracy

If:

* 90 customers stay
* 10 customers leave

Model predicts:

* Everyone will stay

👉 Accuracy = 90% (but useless!)

---

## 🔹 2. Precision

Precision means:

> “Out of all predicted churn customers, how many actually left?”

---

### 🧪 Example

* Predicted churn = 20
* Actually churned = 15

👉 Precision = 15 / 20 = 75%

---

## 🔹 3. Recall

Recall means:

> “Out of all actual churn customers, how many did we correctly detect?”

---

### 🧪 Example

* Actual churn = 20
* Correctly predicted = 15

👉 Recall = 15 / 20 = 75%

---

## 🔹 4. F1 Score

F1 Score combines precision and recall:

> “Balance between precision and recall”

---

### 🧠 Why Needed?

* Precision alone is not enough
* Recall alone is not enough

👉 F1 gives a balanced view

---

## ⚙️ Evaluation in PyTorch

---

### 🧪 Example Code

```python id="4r2fqa"
from sklearn.metrics import accuracy_score, precision_score, recall_score, f1_score

# Convert predictions to 0 or 1
y_pred = (model(X_test) > 0.5).float()

accuracy = accuracy_score(y_test, y_pred)
precision = precision_score(y_test, y_pred)
recall = recall_score(y_test, y_pred)
f1 = f1_score(y_test, y_pred)

print("Accuracy:", accuracy)
print("Precision:", precision)
print("Recall:", recall)
print("F1 Score:", f1)
```

---

### 📊 Confusion Matrix (Basic Idea)

A confusion matrix shows:

* True Positive
* True Negative
* False Positive
* False Negative

👉 It helps understand where the model makes mistakes

---

### 📈 Real-Life Analogy

Think of a medical test:

* Precision → how many detected patients are actually sick
* Recall → how many sick patients were detected

👉 Both are important

---

### 🚀 Key Takeaways

✔ Evaluation checks real-world performance
✔ Accuracy alone is not enough
✔ Precision, Recall, F1 give deeper insight
✔ Helps improve the model
